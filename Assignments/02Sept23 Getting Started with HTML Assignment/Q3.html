Bottom-right: a computer program interpreting a fragment of a clay tablet with cuneiform script about king Shalmaneser III Part of a series on Library and information science OutlineGlossary Histories Focus Curation Interdisciplinary fields Areas WikiProject Category vte A document is a written, drawn, presented, or memorialized representation of thought, often the manifestation of non-fictional, as well as fictional, content. The word originates from the Latin Documentum, which denotes a "teaching" or "lesson": the verb doce≈ç denotes "to teach". In the past, the word was usually used to denote written proof useful as evidence of a truth or fact. In the Computer Age, "document" usually denotes a primarily textual computer file, including its structure and format, e.g. fonts, colors, and images.


The concept of "document" has been defined by Suzanne Briet as "any concrete or symbolic indication, preserved or recorded, for reconstructing or for proving a phenomenon, whether physical or mental."[1]
